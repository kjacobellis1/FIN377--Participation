<p><b>Agilent Technologies, Inc.</b> is a public research, development and manufacturing company established in 1999 as a spin-off from <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>.  The resulting <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a> of Agilent stock was the largest in the history of <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company provides <a href="/wiki/Analytical_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="Analytical instruments">analytical instruments</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a>, services and consumables for the entire laboratory workflow.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Agilent focuses its products and services on six markets: food, environmental and forensics, pharmaceutical, diagnostics, chemical and energy, and research. From 1999 to 2014, the company also produced test and measurement equipment for electronics; that division was spun off to form <a href="/wiki/Keysight" title="Keysight">Keysight</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agilent_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Based on 2003 information, Agilent maintained four locations in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay area</a>: <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rosa,_California" title="Santa Rosa, California">Santa Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rohnert_Park,_California" title="Rohnert Park, California">Rohnert Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Santa Clara is an R&amp;D site,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> containing the Agilent Research Laboratories group.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Based on 2006 information, Agilent maintained seven sites in China: an office in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, and branches in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chengdu" title="Chengdu">Chengdu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi&#39;an">Xi'an</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Agilent also has several manufacturing facilities across Europe, most notably in <a href="/wiki/Waldbronn" title="Waldbronn">Waldbronn</a> <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a> <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">U.K</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Glostrup" title="Glostrup">Glostrup</a> <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_services">Products and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agilent_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products and services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agilent serves analytical laboratories and the clinical and routine diagnostics markets with a full suite of technology platforms. These include: <a href="/wiki/Automation" title="Automation">automation</a>, bioreagents, <a href="/wiki/Fluorescence_in_situ_hybridization" title="Fluorescence in situ hybridization">FISH probes</a>, gas and liquid chromatography, <a href="/wiki/Immunohistochemistry" title="Immunohistochemistry">immunohistochemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Informatics" title="Informatics">informatics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mass_spectrometry" title="Mass spectrometry">mass spectrometry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microarrays" class="mw-redirect" title="Microarrays">microarrays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spectroscopy" title="Spectroscopy">spectroscopy</a>, target enrichment, and vacuum technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-chem.agilent.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chem.agilent.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Agilent also provides the lab management services and lab supplies: <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_asset_management" title="Enterprise asset management">enterprise asset management</a>, laboratory business intelligence, equipment management and service, <a href="/wiki/Software_maintenance" title="Software maintenance">software maintenance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Regulatory_compliance" title="Regulatory compliance">regulatory compliance</a>, sample preparation, genomics and cloning, GC and HPLC columns, spectrometry and spectroscopy supplies, and general laboratory supplies.<sup id="cite_ref-chem.agilent.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chem.agilent.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agilent_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg/220px-Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg/330px-Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg/440px-Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="405" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Agilent-HQ-Lobby.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Agilent Technologies headquarters lobby in Santa Clara, California</div></div></div>
<p>Agilent Technologies was created in 1999 by the spin-off of <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>'s (HP's) "Medical Products and Instrument Group",<sup id="cite_ref-:6_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> including instrumentation and chemical analysis, electronic components, and medical equipment product lines.<sup id="cite_ref-agilentrussia_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-agilentrussia-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup>  The split was predicated on the difficulty of growing HP's revenue stream and on the competitive vigor of smaller, more agile competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The company's launch slogan was "Innovating the HP Way", which capitalized on the strong HP corporate culture.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/Starburst_(symbol)" title="Starburst (symbol)">starburst</a> logo was selected to reflect "a burst of insight" (or "spark of insight")<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the name "Agilent" aimed to invoke the notion of agility as a trait of the new firm.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The Agilent spin-off was accompanied by an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> which raised $2.1 billion, setting a record at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2009">2000–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agilent_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early 2000s, "economic uncertainty" depressed demand for Agilent's products,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> including slow sales of health care products to hospitals in the United States, which accounted for 60% of the company's revenue at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  The downturn also struck sales in the communications and semiconductor markets, where orders amounting to $500 million were canceled by buyers.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  These poor economic conditions prompted large reductions in force; from a headcount in 1999 of 35,000, which had risen to 48,000 by May 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-:7_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> it had by early 2003 cut 18,500 positions.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  In 2001, in midst of this downsizing, Agilent sold its health care and medical products organization to <a href="/wiki/Philips_Medical_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Philips Medical Systems">Philips Medical Systems</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and was noted as having a valuation of about $11 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  HP Medical Products had been the second oldest part of <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>, acquired in the 1950s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In August 2005, Agilent announced the sale of its semiconductor business, which produced chips for a wide range of consumer and industrial uses, to <a href="/wiki/Kohlberg_Kravis_Roberts" title="Kohlberg Kravis Roberts">Kohlberg Kravis Roberts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_Partners" title="Silver Lake Partners">Silver Lake Partners</a> for $2.66 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  This move was part of a broad effort to concentrate "on the test-and-measurement business at its historic core," and would entail termination of about 1,300 of the company's 28,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  The group operated as a private company, <a href="/wiki/Avago_Technologies" class="mw-redirect" title="Avago Technologies">Avago Technologies</a>, until August 2009, when it was brought public in an IPO. After purchasing <a href="/wiki/Broadcom" class="mw-redirect" title="Broadcom">Broadcom Corporation</a> in 2016, Avago changed its name to <a href="/wiki/Broadcom_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Broadcom Limited">Broadcom Limited</a>.
</p><p>Also in August 2005, Agilent sold its 47% stake in the <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">light-emitting diode</a> manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Philips_Lumileds_Lighting_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Philips Lumileds Lighting Company">Lumileds</a> to <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">Philips</a> for $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Lumileds originally started as <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>'s optoelectronics division.
</p><p>Also in August 2005, Agilent announced a plan to divest its semiconductor test solutions business, composed of both the system-on-chip and memory test market areas.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Agilent listed the new company as <a href="/wiki/Verigy" title="Verigy">Verigy</a> on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> in mid-2006.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_onwards">2010 onwards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agilent_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 onwards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Agilent announced the closure of a subsection of its Test &amp; Measurement division. The product lines affected included the automated optical inspection, <a href="/wiki/Solder_paste" title="Solder paste">solder paste</a> inspection, and automated <span class="nowrap">X-ray</span> products [5DX]. In 2004, In 2011, the company, along with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Davis" title="University of California, Davis">University of California, Davis</a>, announced that it would be establishing the "Davis Millimeter Wave Research Center".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Agilent announced it would increase its life sciences engagement through the acquisition of Halo Genomics, based in <a href="/wiki/Uppsala" title="Uppsala">Uppsala</a>, Sweden, which was involved in <a href="/wiki/Next-generation_sequencing" class="mw-redirect" title="Next-generation sequencing">next-generation sequencing</a> technology development.<sup id="cite_ref-dddmag2011_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dddmag2011-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 17, 2012, Agilent agreed to buy Dako, a Danish cancer diagnostics company, for $2.2 billion, to expand its presence in the life sciences industry.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 19, 2013, Agilent announced its decision to separate into two publicly traded companies: Agilent, a life sciences, diagnostics, and applied markets company, and an electronic measurement company.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The life sciences company retained the Agilent name and the electronic measurement company was called <a href="/wiki/Keysight" title="Keysight">Keysight Technologies</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On October 14, 2014, the company announced that it is exiting its Nuclear Magnetic Resonance business.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On November 1, the formal separation of Agilent and <a href="/wiki/Keysight_Technologies" class="mw-redirect" title="Keysight Technologies">Keysight Technologies</a> was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Agilent announced it had completed the spin-off of its electronic measurement business, Keysight Technologies. Keysight began trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol KEYS. The separation was implemented through a spinoff of Keysight's common stock and was intended to be tax-free for U.S. federal income tax purposes. On November 1, 2014, in a special dividend distribution of all outstanding shares of Keysight's common stock, Agilent shareholders received one share of Keysight common stock for every two shares of Agilent common stock held as of close of business October 22, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Agilent celebrated its 50th anniversary in the analytical instruments market. <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard Co.</a>, Agilent's predecessor, acquired F&amp;M Scientific Corp., maker of gas chromatographs, on August 8, 1965. In September 2015, the company announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Seahorse_Bioscience" title="Seahorse Bioscience">Seahorse Bioscience</a> for $235 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 7, 2016, Agilent announced that they had acquired U.K. based Cobalt Light Systems, which develops and manufactures <a href="/wiki/Raman_spectroscopy" title="Raman spectroscopy">Raman spectroscopy</a> instruments, for £40 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In December the company acquired Multiplicom N.V.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2018, the company announced it would acquire Luxcel Biosciences, increasing the company's cell analysis portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In May, Agilent acquired Lasergen, Inc. after the end of its two-year option on a prior investment.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the same month it acquired digital laboratory management company, Genohm,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Ultra Scientific, provider of chemical standards and reference materials<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and Advanced Analytical Technologies, Inc. (AATI), provider of capillary <a href="/wiki/Electrophoresis" title="Electrophoresis">electrophoresis</a>-based molecules for $250 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In August the company announced it would acquire glycan reagent producer, ProZyme, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and South Korean instrument distributor, Young In Scientific Co. Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In September Agilent acquired ACEA Biosciences for $250 million increasing the company's presence in cell analysis technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

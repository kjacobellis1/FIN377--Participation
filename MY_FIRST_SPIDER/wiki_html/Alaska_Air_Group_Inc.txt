<p><b>Alaska Air Group</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_airline_holding_companies" title="List of airline holding companies">airline holding company</a> based in <a href="/wiki/SeaTac,_Washington" title="SeaTac, Washington">SeaTac, Washington</a>. It owns two certificated airlines operating in the United States: <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horizon_Air" title="Horizon Air">Horizon Air</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Oct-2012-8-K_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Oct-2012-8-K-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Alaska Air Group also oversees an aircraft ground handling company, McGee Air Services, which is wholly owned by Alaska Airlines.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alaska_Air_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg/220px-Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="97" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg/330px-Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg/440px-Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6180" data-file-height="2727" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alaska_Airlines_4920s0.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Headquarters for Alaska Air Group</div></div></div>
<p>It was formed in 1985 and acquired Horizon Air and <a href="/wiki/Jet_America_Airlines" title="Jet America Airlines">Jet America Airlines</a> the next year. Jet America Airlines was merged into Alaska Airlines in 1987.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Between 2007 and 2010, the number of employees under Alaska Air Group's subsidiaries dropped from 13,618 (10,142 at Alaska Airlines and 3,476 at Horizon Air) to 12,039 (9,013 at Alaska Airlines and 3,202 at Horizon Air).<sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2008-10-K_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2008-10-K-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2009-10-K_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2009-10-K-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2010-10-K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2010-10-K-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2011-10-K_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2011-10-K-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> By 2011, the number of employees had risen to 12,806 (9,640 at Alaska Airlines and 3,166 at Horizon Air).<sup id="cite_ref-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2012-10-K_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaska-Air-Group-Feb-2012-10-K-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Alaska Air Group replaced the <a href="/wiki/AMR_Corporation" title="AMR Corporation">AMR Corporation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Transportation_Average" title="Dow Jones Transportation Average">Dow Jones Transportation Average</a> following AMR's filing for bankruptcy.<sup id="cite_ref-DowJonesTransportationAvg_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DowJonesTransportationAvg-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 29, 2016 Alaska Airlines announced that it would form a wholly owned subsidiary called McGee Air Services, a dedicated airline services company. McGee competes with other, similar companies to provide ground handling, aircraft cleaning and wheelchair services to Alaska Airlines.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 4, 2016, Alaska Air Group announced plans to acquire <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a>, pending approval from US government regulators and Virgin America shareholders,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> before the acquisition was completed on December 14, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The total price for the acquisition was approximately $2.6 billion. Until 2018, Alaska Air Group continued to operate <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a> as separate airlines and continued to honor both Alaska's Mileage Plan and Virgin America's Elevate loyalty programs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> By the end of 2016, following <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a>'s acquisition the number of Alaska Air Group employees rose to 19,112 (12,224 at Alaska Airlines, 3,616 at Horizon Air, and 3,252 at Virgin America).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 22, 2017 the company announced that Alaska Air Group would merge <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a>, with the combined airline to operate under the Alaska Airlines brand. The merger was largely completed on April 25, 2018 and the Virgin America brand was fully retired by June 2, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alaska_Air_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alaska_Air_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alaska Air Group operates a mix of <a href="/wiki/Airbus" title="Airbus">Airbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Aerospace" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombardier Aerospace">Bombardier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Embraer" title="Embraer">Embraer</a> aircraft through its subsidiaries <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horizon_Air" title="Horizon Air">Horizon Air</a>. As of October 2017, <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a>' fleet consisted of 248 jet aircraft,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Horizon_Air" title="Horizon Air">Horizon Air</a>'s fleet consisted of 41 turboprop and 16 jet aircraft, with the combined fleet under Alaska Air Group's management numbering 298 aircraft.
</p><p>Alaska Air Group has created a new branding identity for its Horizon Air subsidiary and other independently owned and separately directed affiliate <a href="/wiki/Regional_airline" title="Regional airline">regional airlines</a> it chooses to contract to do regional flying business into markets too limited to be flown only on Alaska Airlines <a href="/wiki/Mainline_(flight)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mainline (flight)">mainline</a> equipment. Among the other airlines now sub-contracted to do additional flying for the Alaska Air Group is <a href="/wiki/SkyWest,_Inc." title="SkyWest, Inc.">SkyWest, Inc.</a>'s <a href="/wiki/SkyWest_Airlines" title="SkyWest Airlines">SkyWest Airlines</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_CRJ700_series#CRJ700" title="Bombardier CRJ700 series">Bombardier CRJ700</a> and <a href="/wiki/Embraer_E-Jet_family#E170_and_E175" title="Embraer E-Jet family">Embraer E175</a> aircraft dedicated to providing service for the Alaska Air Group are painted in a very similar manner to Alaska Horizon's. SkyWest's fleet however, is branded <i>Alaska SkyWest</i> to differentiate that airline's aircraft from those of Horizon Air.<sup id="cite_ref-AS_Group_Fleet_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AS_Group_Fleet-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Route_network">Route network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alaska_Air_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Route network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Through <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horizon_Air" title="Horizon Air">Horizon Air</a>, Alaska Air Group services the passenger and cargo markets of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> with its extensive route network hub through <a href="/wiki/Seattle%E2%80%93Tacoma_International_Airport" title="Seattle–Tacoma International Airport">Seattle/Tacoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portland_International_Airport" title="Portland International Airport">Portland International Airports</a>, and the state of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> through <a href="/wiki/Ted_Stevens_Anchorage_International_Airport" title="Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport">Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport</a>. After the demise of <a href="/wiki/Aloha_Airlines" title="Aloha Airlines">Aloha Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/ATA_Airlines" title="ATA Airlines">ATA Airlines</a> in 2008, Alaska Air Group expanded heavily centering on Hawaii and other non-<a href="/wiki/Airline_hub" title="Airline hub">airline hub</a> secondary <a href="/wiki/Lower_48" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower 48">mainland</a> cities and airports, including <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_International_Airport" title="San Diego International Airport">San Diego International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_International_Airport" title="San Jose International Airport">San Jose International Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a> in 2016, Alaska Air Group further expanded into <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> through <a href="/wiki/Virgin_America" title="Virgin America">Virgin America</a>'s hubs at <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_International_Airport" title="San Francisco International Airport">San Francisco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_International_Airport" title="Los Angeles International Airport">Los Angeles International Airports</a>, and focus city at <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Love_Field" title="Dallas Love Field">Dallas Love Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><b>Accenture plc</b>, stylised as <b>accenture</b>, is an Irish-domiciled <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_services" title="Professional services">professional services</a> company that provides services in strategy, consulting, digital, technology and operations. A <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Global_500" title="Fortune Global 500">Fortune Global 500</a> company,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> it has been incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> since September 1, 2009. In 2019, the company reported revenues of $43.2 billion, with more than 492,000 employees<sup id="cite_ref-Q4_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q4-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> serving clients in more than 200 cities in 120 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-Financial_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Financial-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, the company had about 150,000 employees in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 48,000 in the <a href="/wiki/US" class="mw-redirect" title="US">US</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Marek16_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marek16-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and 50,000 in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Accenture's current clients include 91 of the Fortune Global 100 and more than three-quarters of the Fortune Global 500.<sup id="cite_ref-FactSheet20_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FactSheet20-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 11, 2019, Accenture appointed <a href="/wiki/Julie_Sweet" title="Julie Sweet">Julie Sweet</a> as their new Chief Executive Officer. She accepted her office on September 1, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_early_years">Formation and early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Accenture began as the business and technology consulting division of accounting firm <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Andersen" title="Arthur Andersen">Arthur Andersen</a><sup id="cite_ref-WBBM11_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBBM11-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> in the early 1950s when it conducted a feasibility study for <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> to install a computer at <a href="/wiki/Appliance_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Appliance Park">Appliance Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Brachear05_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brachear05-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> which led to GE's installation of a <a href="/wiki/UNIVAC_I" title="UNIVAC I">UNIVAC I</a> computer and printer,<sup id="cite_ref-Betts01_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Betts01-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> believed to be the first commercial use of a computer in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-Betts90_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Betts90-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Glickauf" title="Joseph Glickauf">Joseph Glickauf</a>, an early pioneer of computer consulting, held a position as head of Arthur Andersen's administrative services division.<sup id="cite_ref-Brachear05_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brachear05-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Split_from_Arthur_Andersen">Split from Arthur Andersen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Split from Arthur Andersen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1989, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Andersen" title="Arthur Andersen">Arthur Andersen</a> and Andersen Consulting became separate units of <a href="/wiki/Andersen_Worldwide_Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Coop%C3%A9rative" class="mw-redirect" title="Andersen Worldwide Société Coopérative">Andersen Worldwide Société Coopérative</a> (AWSC). Throughout the 1990s, there was increasing tension between Andersen Consulting and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Andersen" title="Arthur Andersen">Arthur Andersen</a>. Andersen Consulting was paying Arthur Andersen up to 15% of its profits each year (a provision of the 1989 split was that the more profitable unit – whether AA or AC – pay the other the 15 percent), while at the same time Arthur Andersen was competing with Andersen Consulting through its own newly established business consulting service line called Arthur Andersen Business Consulting (AABC). This dispute came to a head in 1998 when Andersen Consulting put the 15% transfer payment for that year and future years into <a href="/wiki/Escrow" title="Escrow">escrow</a> and issued a claim for breach of contract against AWSC and Arthur Andersen. In August 2000, as a result of the conclusion of arbitration with the <a href="/wiki/International_Chamber_of_Commerce" title="International Chamber of Commerce">International Chamber of Commerce</a>, Andersen Consulting broke all contractual ties with AWSC and Arthur Andersen. As part of the arbitration settlement, Andersen Consulting paid the sum held in escrow (then $1.2&#160;billion) to Arthur Andersen, and was required to change its name, resulting in the entity being renamed Accenture.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergence_of_Accenture">Emergence of Accenture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Emergence of Accenture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 January 2001, Andersen Consulting adopted its current name, "Accenture". The word "Accenture" is derived from "Accent on the future". The name "Accenture" was submitted by Kim Petersen, a Danish employee from the company's Oslo, Norway office, as a result of an internal competition. Andersen felt that the name should represent its will to be a global consulting leader and high performer, and also intended that the name should not be offensive in any country in which Accenture operates.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AccentureNYSE.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/AccentureNYSE.jpg/220px-AccentureNYSE.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/AccentureNYSE.jpg/330px-AccentureNYSE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/AccentureNYSE.jpg/440px-AccentureNYSE.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AccentureNYSE.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Accenture's banner hanging on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> (<a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a>) building for its initial public offering on 19 July 2001.</div></div></div>
<p>On 19 July 2001, Accenture's initial public offering (<a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a>) was priced at $14.50 per share, and the shares began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> (NYSE); <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> served as its lead underwriters. Accenture stock closed the day at $15.17, with the day's high at $15.25. On the first day of the IPO, Accenture raised nearly $1.7&#160;billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s:_Bermuda_headquarters">2000s: Bermuda headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s: Bermuda headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2002, the Congressional <a href="/wiki/Government_Accountability_Office" title="Government Accountability Office">General Accounting Office</a> (GAO) identified Accenture as one of four publicly traded federal contractors that were incorporated in a tax haven.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The other three, unlike Accenture, were incorporated in the United States before they re-incorporated in a tax haven, thereby lowering their US taxes. Critics, most notably former <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> journalist <a href="/wiki/Lou_Dobbs" title="Lou Dobbs">Lou Dobbs</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> have reported Accenture's decision to incorporate in Bermuda as a US <a href="/wiki/Tax_avoidance" title="Tax avoidance">tax avoidance</a> ploy, because they viewed Accenture as having been a US-based company.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The GAO itself did not characterize Accenture as having been a US-based company; it stated that "prior to incorporating in Bermuda, Accenture was operating as a series of related partnerships and corporations under the control of its partners through the mechanism of contracts with a Swiss coordinating entity."<sup id="cite_ref-GAO_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAO-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Accenture engaged in an <a href="/wiki/NHS_Connecting_for_Health" title="NHS Connecting for Health">IT overhaul project</a> for the <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">National Health Service</a> (NHS) in 2003, making headlines when it withdrew from the contract in 2006 over disputes related to delays and cost overruns.<sup id="cite_ref-nhswithdraw_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nhswithdraw-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The government of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> ultimately abandoned the project five years later for the same reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-nhscancel_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nhscancel-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Accenture,_Georgia_Tech.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg/220px-Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="391" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg/330px-Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg/440px-Accenture%2C_Georgia_Tech.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2268" data-file-height="4032" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Accenture,_Georgia_Tech.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Accenture office at <a href="/wiki/Technology_Square_(Atlanta)" title="Technology Square (Atlanta)">Tech Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a></div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s:_Ireland_headquarters_and_recent_history">2010s: Ireland headquarters and recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s: Ireland headquarters and recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Accenture announced on 26 May 2009 that its board of directors unanimously approved changing the company's place of incorporation from <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and would become <b>Accenture plc</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012 it was revealed Accenture was paying only 3.5% in tax in the Republic of Ireland as opposed to the average rate of 24%.<sup id="cite_ref-CompWeek12_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CompWeek12-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Accenture was chosen to replace <a href="/wiki/CGI_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="CGI Group">CGI Group</a> as the lead contractor for <a href="/wiki/HealthCare.gov" title="HealthCare.gov">HealthCare.gov</a> in January 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-healthgov_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-healthgov-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In December 2014, Accenture won a $563 million contract to provide ongoing maintenance, software development and technology support for HealthCare.gov through 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2015, the United States Department of Defense awarded a major <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Health_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic Health Records">Electronic Health Records</a> contract to <a href="/wiki/Cerner" title="Cerner">Cerner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leidos" title="Leidos">Leidos</a> and Accenture. The contract valued $4.33 billion will serve 55 hospitals and 600 clinics. Accenture Federal Services and Leidos will play the role of configuration specialist, while Cerner is the prime contractor.<sup id="cite_ref-Mukherjee_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mukherjee-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 August 2017, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc</a>. announced a partnership with Accenture to create <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> business software.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2018, Accenture generated controversy over the amount the firm has been charging to recruit 7,500 <a href="/wiki/U.S._Customs_and_Border_Protection" title="U.S. Customs and Border Protection">Customs and Border Protection</a> officers. Under the $297 million contract, Accenture had been charging the US Government nearly $40,000 per hire, which is more than the annual salary of the average officer.<sup id="cite_ref-Lanard_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lanard-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  According to a report published by the DHS Office of Inspector General in December 2018, Accenture had been paid $13.6M through the first ten months of the contract. They had hired two agents against a contract goal of 7,500 hires over 5 years. The report was issued as a 'management alert', indicating an issue requiring immediate attention, stating that "Accenture has already taken longer to deploy and delivered less capability than promised".<sup id="cite_ref-Sands_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sands-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2019, CEO Pierre Nanterme stepped down from his position, citing health reasons. Twenty days after stepping down, Nanterme died in France at the age of 59 after being diagnosed with colon cancer. Chief Financial Officer David Rowland was named as the interim CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-death_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2019, <a href="/wiki/Independent_contractor" title="Independent contractor">contractors</a> from Accenture's <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a> location who performed <a href="/wiki/Content_moderation" class="mw-redirect" title="Content moderation">content moderation</a> tasks for <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> wrote an <a href="/wiki/Open_letter" title="Open letter">open letter</a> to Facebook describing poor working conditions and a "<a href="/wiki/Big_Brother_(Nineteen_Eighty-Four)" title="Big Brother (Nineteen Eighty-Four)">Big Brother</a> environment" that included restricted <a href="/wiki/Break_(work)" title="Break (work)">work breaks</a> and strict <a href="/wiki/Non-disclosure_agreement" title="Non-disclosure agreement">non-disclosure agreements</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BI_moderators_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BI_moderators-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WaPo_moderators_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo_moderators-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> A counselor in the Austin office stated that the content moderators could develop <a href="/wiki/Posttraumatic_stress_disorder" title="Posttraumatic stress disorder">posttraumatic stress disorder</a> as a result of the work, which included evaluating videos and images containing <a href="/wiki/Graphic_violence" title="Graphic violence">graphic violence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hate_speech" title="Hate speech">hate speech</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animal_abuse" class="mw-redirect" title="Animal abuse">animal abuse</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Child_abuse" title="Child abuse">child abuse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WaPo_moderators_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo_moderators-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2019, <a href="/wiki/Julie_Sweet" title="Julie Sweet">Julie Sweet</a>, previously CEO of Accenture North America, was named the new chief executive officer of the firm, effective September 2019. She replaced the interim CEO, David Rowland.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 7 January 2020, news sources reported that Accenture had agreed to acquire Symantec's 300-person cybersecurity services division from Broadcom.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2020, Accenture announced that it plans to shut down its media auditing by the end of August.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Finances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the fiscal year 2019, Accenture reported earnings of US$6.305 billion, with an annual revenue of US$43.215 billion, an increase of 5.4% over the previous fiscal cycle. Accenture's shares traded at over $197 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at over US$125.1 billion in November 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Marketing.2C_branding_and_identity"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing,_branding_and_identity">Marketing, branding and identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accenture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing, branding and identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Accenture launched a new campaign of results-based advertisements featuring clients such as <a href="/wiki/Marriott_International" title="Marriott International">Marriott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> alongside its slogan "High performance Delivered".<sup id="cite_ref-Elliott11_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elliott11-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> As of 2019, Interbrand ranked Accenture No. 31 on its list of best global brands.<sup id="cite_ref-Interbrand19_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interbrand19-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The brand consultancy noted Accenture's focus on <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">branding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> of its Accenture Strategy, Accenture Consulting, Accenture Digital, Accenture Technology and Accenture Operations divisions.<sup id="cite_ref-Interbrand_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interbrand-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From at least 2005<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> until December 2009, Accenture used <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> as a celebrity spokesperson and advertised using the <a href="/wiki/Service_mark" title="Service mark">service mark</a> "Go on, be a Tiger" and the ancillary statement "We know what it takes to be a Tiger" in association with his image. On 13 December 2009 after details of <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods#Infidelity_scandal_and_fallout" title="Tiger Woods">Woods' extra-marital affairs</a> were exposed, the company terminated Woods' six-year sponsorship deal.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company uses a standardised system of branding, with extensive use of the font <a href="/wiki/Graphik_(typeface)" class="mw-redirect" title="Graphik (typeface)">Graphik</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Underconsideration.com_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Underconsideration.com-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1999, the firm's culture was parodied by the webcomic <i>Bigtime Consulting</i>, operated pseudonymously by its San Francisco-based employee James Sanchez.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Web_satire_causes_Bigtime_headaches_for_Andersen_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Web_satire_causes_Bigtime_headaches_for_Andersen-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Accenture has implemented policies to reduce gendered discrimination such as gender neutral bathrooms and gender neutral dress-codes.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2019, Accenture is currently engaged in a lawsuit with <a href="/wiki/The_Hertz_Corporation" title="The Hertz Corporation">The Hertz Corporation</a>.  Hertz sued Accenture in April over a contract where Accenture was hired to build a website and app for Hertz.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>  On September 12, both parties appeared before the judge and provided oral arguments on the legitimacy of Hertz's complaint and Accenture's motion to dismiss.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> On November 15, fact discovery is likely to be completed according to schedule, with expert witness depositions finished by January 31, 2020, and a pre-trial conference on February 21.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><b>AbbVie</b> is an American publicly traded <a href="/wiki/Biopharmaceutical" title="Biopharmaceutical">biopharmaceutical</a> company founded in 2013. It originated as a <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin-off</a> of <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Laboratories" title="Abbott Laboratories">Abbott Laboratories</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AbbVie_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 19, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Laboratories" title="Abbott Laboratories">Abbott Laboratories</a> announced its plan to separate into two publicly traded companies. The new Abbott Laboratories would specialize in diversified products including medical devices, diagnostic equipment and nutrition products, while AbbVie would operate as a research-based pharmaceutical manufacturer.<sup id="cite_ref-The_New_York_Times_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_New_York_Times-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The separation was effective January 1, 2013, and AbbVie was officially listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> (ABBV) on January 2, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-AbbVie_Press_Release_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbbVie_Press_Release-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Miles White, CEO at the time, the purpose of the split was to allow markets to value the two businesses separately; White said that investors would "benefit from two fundamentally different investment opportunities with distinct strategic profiles and business priorities."<sup id="cite_ref-Chicago_Tribune_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chicago_Tribune-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Some investors were concerned that the split was done to protect the value of the device business from the loss of value facing the drug division due to the imminent expiration of patents on <a href="/wiki/Humira" class="mw-redirect" title="Humira">Humira</a>, which accounted for about half of the drug division's revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-Chicago_Tribune_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chicago_Tribune-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of December 2015, the company employed in excess of 28,000 globally, and provided products to individuals in more than 170 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AbbVie_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2014, the company acquired ImmuVen for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On September 3, 2014, AbbVie and Infinity Pharmaceuticals announced that they had entered into a global collaboration to develop and commercialize <a href="/wiki/Duvelisib" title="Duvelisib">duvelisib</a>, Infinity's PI3K inhibitor for the treatment of patients with cancer. On the same day, AbbVie and <a href="/wiki/Calico_(company)" title="Calico (company)">Calico</a> announced that they had entered into a <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">R&amp;D</a> collaboration intended to discover, develop and bring to market new therapies for patients with age-related diseases including <a href="/wiki/Neurodegeneration" title="Neurodegeneration">neurodegeneration</a> and cancer. Calico (California Life Company) is an <a href="/wiki/Alphabet_Inc." title="Alphabet Inc.">Alphabet Inc.</a> subsidiary led by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_D._Levinson" title="Arthur D. Levinson">Arthur D. Levinson</a> (former Chairman and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Genentech" title="Genentech">Genentech</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Hal_V._Barron" title="Hal V. Barron">Hal V. Barron</a> (former Executive Vice President and Chief Medical Officer of Genentech) that is focused on aging and age-related diseases.<sup id="cite_ref-Genetic_Engineering_&amp;_Biotechnology_News_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genetic_Engineering_&amp;_Biotechnology_News-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2014, after a long negotiation, AbbVie stopped its efforts to acquire <a href="/wiki/Shire_(pharmaceutical_company)" title="Shire (pharmaceutical company)">Shire</a>, which would have been one of the largest M&amp;A deals of that year and one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Tax_inversion" title="Tax inversion">tax inversions</a> in history, due to changes in the US tax code by the US Treasury; AbbVie had to pay a $1.6 billion breakup fee.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 4, 2015, AbbVie announced its agreement to acquire the <a href="/wiki/Oncology" title="Oncology">oncology</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Pharmacyclics" title="Pharmacyclics">Pharmacyclics</a> and its treatment for blood cancers, <a href="/wiki/Ibrutinib" title="Ibrutinib">ibrutinib</a>; <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a> had also been bidding to acquire Pharmacyclics.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Under the terms of the transaction, AbbVie agreed to pay $261.25 per share as a mix of cash and AbbVie equity. The acquisition valued at approximately $21 billion was completed on May 26, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Pharmacyclics name was retained, and it operates as a subsidiary of AbbVie from its previous <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>, headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-StreetInsider.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StreetInsider.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  On June 3, 2015, AbbVie and Halozyme Therapeutics announced that they had entered into a global collaboration and licensing agreement to develop and commercialize products that combine AbbVie’s treatments and Halozyme’s ENHANZE drug-delivery technology, this was terminated in November 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 April 2016, the company announced it would acquire Stemcentrx for up to $9.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> A day later, the company announced an expansion of a two and a half-year-old cystic fibrosis deal with <a href="/wiki/Galapagos_NV" title="Galapagos NV">Galapagos</a>, potentially doubling milestone payments to $600 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 25 June 2019, AbbVie announced it would acquire Irish-based <a href="/wiki/Allergan_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Allergan plc">Allergan plc</a> for about $63 billion; however the transaction would not be structured as a <a href="/wiki/Tax_inversion" title="Tax inversion">tax inversion</a>, and that post the transaction, AbbVie (and the group) would remain legally domiciled in the U.S. for tax purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-abbvieguardian_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbvieguardian-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-abbviebloomberg_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbviebloomberg-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In July, the company announced it would acquire Mavupharma, boosting its cancer drug pipeline.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaboration">Collaboration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AbbVie_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Collaboration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 10, 2016, AbbVie and <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>-based Synlogic announced a multi-year R&amp;D collaboration. Synlogic is a synthetic biology company built on research from the labs of <a href="/wiki/James_Collins_(bioengineer)" title="James Collins (bioengineer)">James Collins</a> and Tim Lu at <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a>. As part of the collaboration, AbbVie is getting worldwide rights to Synlogic’s <a href="/wiki/Probiotic" title="Probiotic">probiotic</a>-based technology for treating <a href="/wiki/Inflammatory_bowel_disease" title="Inflammatory bowel disease">inflammatory bowel disease</a>, and the research teams will focus on <a href="/wiki/Crohn%E2%80%99s_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Crohn’s disease">Crohn’s disease</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulcerative_colitis" title="Ulcerative colitis">ulcerative colitis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In April 2016, the company partnered with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> to investigate a number of areas of oncology: breast, lung, prostate, colorectal and hematological cancers.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the same month the company announced it would co-commercialize Argenx's preclinical immunotherapy, ARGX-115. ARGX-115 is a first-in-class immunotherapy targeting GARP (glycoprotein A repetitions predominant), a membrane protein believed to enhance the immunosuppressive effects of <a href="/wiki/T_cells" class="mw-redirect" title="T cells">T cells</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The company also announced a deal to co-develop/commercialize at least one of CytomX Probody's conjugates against <a href="/wiki/CD71" class="mw-redirect" title="CD71">CD71</a> (transferrin receptor 1).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AbbVie_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a> as of January 2016 <a href="/wiki/Ibrutinib" title="Ibrutinib">ibrutinib</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Specialty_drug" class="mw-redirect" title="Specialty drug">specialty drug</a>, cost US$116,600 to $155,400 a year wholesale in the United States. In spite of discounts and medical insurance, the prohibitive price causes some patients to not fill their prescriptions.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_2016_jan_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_2016_jan-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> AbbVie estimates global sales of the drug at $1 billion in 2016 and $5 billion in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_2016_jan_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_2016_jan-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018 it started litigation against <a href="/wiki/NHS_England" title="NHS England">NHS England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Technology_and_Construction_Court" title="Technology and Construction Court">Technology and Construction Court</a> claiming that they breached procurement rules and had not treated the company fairly during what was described as "the single largest medicines procurement ever done by the NHS" when seeking suppliers for <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">hepatitis C</a> treatments.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, a UK court dismissed AbbVie's case against the NHS. <sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2019, the company remained committed to product development in the <a href="/wiki/Cystic_fibrosis" title="Cystic fibrosis">cystic fibrosis</a> disease area, despite major success by their main competitor in the area, <a href="/wiki/Vertex_Pharmaceuticals" title="Vertex Pharmaceuticals">Vertex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
